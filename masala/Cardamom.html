CARDAMOM

Botanical Name: Elettaria cardamomum

Sun Exposure: Full Sun

Plant Type: Fruit

Soil pH: Neutral

Bloom Time: Summer, Fall

Flower Color: Pink

Hardiness Zones: 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8

Where We Grow

Site Selection

  • Altitude: Cardamom thrives in elevations of 600-1500 meters above sea level.
  • Shade: Choose a site with partial shade from taller trees.
  • Soil: Well-drained, fertile, loamy soil rich in organic matter with a slightly acidic pH (5.5-6.5).

Land Preparation

  • Clearing: Remove weeds and existing vegetation from the land.
  • Tilling: Thoroughly plow the soil to loosen it.
  • Drainage: Create drainage channels if your soil is prone to waterlogging.
  • Manure: Mix compost or well-rotted manure into the soil.

Climate

  • Rainfall: Ideal annual rainfall of 1500-2500 mm, well-distributed throughout the year.
  • Temperature: Optimal range between 10°C and 35°C.
  • Humidity: High humidity is preferred.

How We Grow

Lorem

  • Propagation: Cardamom is primarily grown using suckers (rhizome divisions) obtained from mature plants.
  • Planting: Plant suckers in pits (approx. 60cm x 60cm x 60cm) with a spacing of 1.5-3 meters. Plant during the rainy season.
  • Mulching: Cover the base of the plants with mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds.

Care And Maintenance

Irrigation

  • Irrigation: Provide regular irrigation, especially during dry periods.

Pruning

  • Pruning: Remove old and dried-up leaves and stems periodically.

Pest and Disease Control

  • Pest and Disease Control: Monitor for common pests like thrips and aphids, and fungal diseases. Use organic control methods or consult an agricultural expert.

Weeding

  • Weeding: Keep the area around the plants weed-free.

Fertilizer

  • Fertilizer: Apply a balanced mix of compost, cow dung manure, and NPK fertilizers regularly.

Harvesting

  • Maturity: Cardamom plants begin producing capsules after 2-3 years.
  • Harvesting: Pick capsules individually when they turn a deep green color and the seeds turn black.
  • Drying: Dry the capsules in the sun or in a mechanical dryer.

Storage and Control

  • Storage: Store dried capsules in airtight containers in a cool, dark place.
