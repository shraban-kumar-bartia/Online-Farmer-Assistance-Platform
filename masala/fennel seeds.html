FENNEL SEEDS

Botanical Name:Foeniculum vulgare

Sun Exposure: Full Sun

Plant Type: Fruit

Soil pH: Neutral

Bloom Time: Summer, Fall

Flower Color: Pink

Hardiness Zones: 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8

Where We Grow

Site Selection

  • Well-drained Soil: Fennel prefers fertile, well-drained soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH (6.0-7.0). Avoid heavy clay soils prone to waterlogging.
  • Sunlight: Choose a site with full sun exposure for optimal growth and seed production.

Land Preparation

  • Thorough Tillage: Plough the land thoroughly and harrow to create a fine, level seedbed.
  • Organic Matter: Incorporate well-rotted compost or manure to improve the soil structure and fertility.

Climate

  • Cool and Dry: Fennel thrives in a cool and dry climate for optimal seed production. It can tolerate some frost.
  • Temperature: Ideal temperature range for fennel seed production is 15-20°C (59-68°F).

  • Sowing Method: Fennel seeds can be sown directly in the field or transplanted from a nursery bed.
  • Sowing Time: The best time for sowing fennel seeds in India is during the Rabi season (October-November).
  • Spacing: Maintain a row-to-row spacing of 45 cm and a plant-to-plant spacing of 30 cm.
  • Seed Depth: Sow seeds at a depth of 0.5-1 cm.

Care And Maintenance

Irrigation

  • Irrigation: Irrigate the crop regularly, especially during dry periods. Avoid overwatering, which can lead to root rot.

Pruning

  • Pruning: Remove any flowering stems as they appear to encourage vegetative growth and better seed yield.

Pest and Disease Control

  • Pest and Disease Control: Monitor for common pests like aphids and mites, and diseases like powdery mildew. Use suitable control measures.

Weeding

  • Weeding: Keep the field weed-free to reduce competition for resources.

Fertilizer

  • Fertilizer: Apply a balanced fertilizer after 30-45 days of sowing.

Harvesting

  • Maturity: Fennel seeds are ready for harvest when they turn from green to brown, approximately 160-180 days after sowing.
  • Harvesting Method: Cut the umbels (seed heads) carefully with a sickle or shears.
  • Threshing and Drying: Thresh the umbels to separate the seeds and dry them thoroughly in the sun.

Storage and Control

  • Storage: Store the cleaned and dried fennel seeds in airtight containers in a cool, dark place.
